Synopsis
A deity from a ancillary shrine summarizes the events from the first act, gives additional accounts of famous swords, and expresses confidence in the power of prayer and Munechika's ability to complete the task.
Text
Prose.
Music
None.
Visuals
The kyōgen actor wearing a traditional kyōgen garb for a subsidiary deity and a noborihige mask delivers his monologue standing at Square 1.

The kyōgen actor personifies a deity, that is why he is masked and his entrance accompanied with music. In Kokaji's case, he enters the stage over the music of raijo. The intermedia character of the interlude is simple, as it is usually the case. The actor recites his text with very little movement and no music.

Often during the interlude, also called the nakairi, the kyōgen actor has a discussion with the waki. In such instances, he kneels center stage and talks with the waki, who is kneeling at Square 5, as it is the case in Hashitomi’s aikyōgen. But in Kokaji, the waki has exited the stage with the shite for a change of costume, so the kyōgen actor recites a monologue while standing on Square 1. Like the members of the jiutai, the four musicians have their hands in their pockets to show that although on stage, they are not part of the action.

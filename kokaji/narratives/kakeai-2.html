Form
Second Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-2 → Kiri
Synopsis
Munechika and the deity complete the forging of the blade by inscribing their names: "Munechika the Swordsmith" on one side, and "Little Fox" on the other.
Text
Prose.
Music
An exchange between shite and waki delivered in kotoba, accompanied by ōtsuzumi and kotsuzumi in flexible rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The two protagonists remain on the dais located on Square 4 and mimick inscribing their names on the blade with the hammers.

The kakeai-2 is like a moment of respite before the play's dynamic conclusion. The text, which is matter-of-fact, is spoken by the shite and waki, rather than sung. Also, the removal of the nohkan, taiko, and jiutai reduces the instrumental density. Finally, the strict rhythmic synchronization of the chant and its accompaniment found in the preceding five shōdan is temporarily interrupted, although it will return in the next and last shōdan, the kiri. These factors contribute to produce a significant drop in the Intermedia Index.

When it comes to the coordination between text and kata, the kakeai-2 is very much a continuation of the noriji-3. The audience hears and sees the two performers completing the forging of the blade by engraving their respective signatures.

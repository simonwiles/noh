Form
First Act - Dialogue:
Mondō-2 → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
As Munechika walks to the Inari Shrine to pray for divine assistance in forging the blade, a mysterious young boy appears out of nowhere and surprises Munechika by knowing about the Imperial order.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the shite and waki delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The waki, standing on Square 6, turns toward the mirror room. The curtain being lifted, he hears the voice of the shite, who then enters the bridge and walks to Square 1.

Although the Intermedia Index of this spoken dialogue remains low, there is an interesting intermedia quality that comes from the use of stage space and the setting of the text.

Kokaji’s first act does not have a 'Shite Enters' dan since his appearance is a surprise, an effect that would have been spoiled with an entrance music. Instead, the shōdan opens with the shite's first line of a dialogue. It comes as a yobikake, a call from the distance, when the shite is still out of sight in the mirror room. The waki, standing on Square 6 and facing in his direction, is mesmerized, and wonders about who is addressing him. Then, the shite enters and engages in a dialogue with the waki, separated from him by the widest possible distance, which creates the dramatic effect of being worlds apart.

As the shite in the role of a young boy finally reaches Square 1 and turns to face Munechika, the tone and style of delivery of the last two lines of the shōdan change suddenly. The actors switch to non-congruent singing to share a sentence of poetic text. The waki says a line that summarizes his astonishment : "A voice in heaven." It is followed with the shite’s "echoes on the earth," which confirms his arrival in Munechika’s world. This switch also serves as a smooth transition to the next shōdan, whose poetic text is also sung.

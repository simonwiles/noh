Form
Second Act - Shite Performs:
Noriji-2 → Maibataraki → Noriji-3
Synopsis
The deity invites Munechika to join him and together they begin to forge the Imperial blade.
Text
Poetry in irregular rhythm.
Music
The jiutai sings in a text set in ōnori in tsuyogin style, accompanied by all three percussion instruments in strict rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The shite and waki walk to Square 4 where they mount the dais, kneel, and take turns at mimicking the forging of a blade with a striking kata.

Although the Intermedia Index drops slightly because the nohkan is not involved in the shōdan, overall it remains very high because of the richness of movement and congruency of the music. The shite and jiutai alternate as narrators, describing the strongly mimetic action of sword forging the audience is watching. Text and kata are in perfect synchrony, as motions visualize the text, which itself involves onomatopea to strengthen the 'realistic' quality of the scene.

The noriji-3 can be divided into three segments, each beginning the taiko's head pattern kashira. In the first segment we are told that the deity is a young boy who ascends the forge mound before bowing to the waki. During the next segment, the deity asks Munechika to bring the metal to be forged into a blade. He obliges, and as they proceed with their work, by alternating hammering, they imitated the sound of the hit metal with the onomatopoeic: ‘chō’. In the third segment, the text highlights the magnificence of the event. As the jiutai narrates that the strokes sound through heaven and earth, the shite jumps out of the dais, covering his head with his sleeves to indicate that he has temporarily disappeared from the location. His Up-Left-Right-Down Head motion suggests, however, that he can hear the sound of the strokes bouncing off through space. This is followed by the waki’s Left-Right Head motion, confirming that he can also hear it.

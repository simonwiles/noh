Form
Second Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-2 → Kiri
Synopsis
The sword, called 'Clustering Clouds of Heaven', is celebrated with a dance. The deity proclaims the end of its work, offers the sword to the Imperial envoy, and jumps on a cloud to return to Mt. Inari.
Text
Poetry in irregular rhythm.
Music
The jiutai sings a chant in tsuyogin style, with one intervention by the shite, set in chūnori and hiranori. It is accompanied by kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi in strict and nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings.
Visuals
The dance of this dance-to-text shōdan uses most of the stage space. The shite takes the blade, performs a few kata holding it, then brings it to the envoy while the waki watches them. The envoy accepts the blade, the shite bows and performs a short sequence of kata, ending by walking onto the bridge and stopping at the Second Pine where he mimes his disappearance.

The final shōdan of the play involves active dance, congruent singing, percussion in strict rhythmic setting, and a striking closing pattern by the nohkan. This brings the Intermedia Index to its second highest point. The mimetic movement matches the text, and the fast music underlines the climactic character of the section.

The kiri can be divided into three parts: introducing the sword as 'Clustering Clouds of Heaven', boasting of its powers as it is offered to the envoy, and the deity’s exit.

To emphasize the name of the sword, an inserted measure of four-beat (toriji) interrupts the steady flow of the honji. It creates a pause that stresses the name “Ama no murakumo…” ("Clustering Clouds of Heaven..."). The deity shows the sword by performing the standard sequence of kata: Forward Pointing and Open-retreat, while holding the blade up high at arm's length.

The second part begins with the deity looking at the sword held between stretched arms and pronouncing: 'First under heaven'. Then, the jiutai repeats the claim and tells about the protection and blessings that the sword will bring. The dance’s momentum picks up until the end of the kiri. The shite reinforces the statements with a series of Six and Seven Stamps, which produces a dynamic counterpoint with the melodic line. The text that follows speaks of ruling the land to the four seas and the abundance of the five staple crops. It is mimicked with a loosely synchronized sequence of formal kata: Pointing and Large Circle . As the jiutai sings, “Inari no shintai Kogitsune-maru wo/The manifestation of Inari: the Little Fox,” the shite prepares to formally deliver the blade by performing an Open-retreat that brings him back to Square 1. In an intense moment, the deity, holding the sword with two hands, walks towards the Emperor's envoy, hands the blade to him, moves back, Kneel, and Bow.

The final section of the text starts, "kore made nari to ii-sutete/Now, I am done!", and brings a change of tone created by a sudden spike in tempo of both music and dance. The deity stands up and performs several virtuosic kata with spectacular agility: Two Large Steps, a Leap Turn, and a sequence of Kneeling that mimic the words "mata mura-kumo ni tobi-nori/he leaps upon the mass of clouds." The closing nohkan’s tome no te pattern confirms the finality of the situation as the deity moves with determination to the Second Pine on the Bridge. Once more he takes Two Large Steps in preparation for a Leap Turn, illustrating his return to Mt. Inari, as the jiutai sings: "Higashiyama Inari no mine ni zo kaerikeru/and returns to the Eastern Peak of Mt. Inari!." Finally, the deity flips his left sleeve over his head to indicate his disappearance from view, while Munechika keeps his gaze into the distance, as if seeing the deity off.

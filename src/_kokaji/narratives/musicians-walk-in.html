The hayashi and jiutai slowly enter and take their respective positions in the back and on stage-left.

The musicians enter in silence. The curtain is pulled back partially on one side for the instrumentatlists. The full curtain rise is reserved for the actors whose entrance is sometimes dramatized. Traditionally, the musicians' role has been described as mere accompaniment of the actors, thus they appear with humility and poise. They enter one at a time and walk slowly over the bridge to take their position. The jiutai members enter through a small door on stage-left and with similar modesty and decorum take their sitting positions on the side of the stage. The performers avoid turning their backs to the audience or making eye contact. They preserve the kurai introduced during the oshirabe.

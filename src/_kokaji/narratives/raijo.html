Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-1 → Ageuta-3 → Raijo
Synopsis
Exit of the shite and waki followed by the entrance of the kyōgen actor.
Text
None.
Music
The full hayashi performs the raijo. For exit of the two protagonists, the music includes a percussion part in flexible and a nohkan part in non-congruent rhythmic settings. For the entrance of the kyōgen, the percussion part seamlessly change to strict rhythmic setting, while the nohkan part remains non-congruent.
Visuals
The shite takes over two minutes to exit. When he reaches the Second Pine, the waki stands up and follows him. After they both have left, the kyōgen enters. The curtain rises for each of them individually.

The Ageuta 3, concluded with the nohkan's shrilling hishigi pattern, setting up an exciting tone for the deity’s vanishing. The raijo releases that energy.

The slow and non-congruent first part accompanies the exit of the two protagonists, while its congruent second part accompanies the kyōgen’s entrance. The shite is the first one to leave. The waki follows, but he does not enter the bridge until the shite has left. The curtain rises and lowers for the exit of each actor. It rises one last time for the entrance of the kyōgen actor.

The nohkan is the featured instrument, accompanied by the three percussion instruments whose respective rhythmic patterns merge into a single macro-rhythm. Since one cannot predict how long the exit and entrance of an actor will last, musicians follow the mihakarai principle. This is a concept of temporal flexibility in which the number of repetitions of a pattern is determined by the action on stage. In this case, the time the actors take to exit and enter the stage determines the number of repetitions. The taiko player, who sits in front of the bridge, introduces a sequence of three shōnobachi strokes on the honji's seventh beat, and shortens his kakegoe to communicate to the other musicians the move to congruency and increase in tempo.

At the beginning of the raijo, the shite takes three special footsteps, each performed solemnly, synchronized with the kakegoe ‘ho’ on beats 2.5 and 6.5. Each step symbolizes the shite traveling a long distance at a very fast speed. After the third step, the shite walks out in standard fashion.

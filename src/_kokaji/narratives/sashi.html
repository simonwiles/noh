Form
First Act - Shite Performs:
Kuri → Sashi → Kuse
Synopsis
The young boy tells the story of a late minister's soul giving power to the emperor Huan Tsung's blade.
Text
Arhythmic poetry.
Music
The shite and jiutia sing this sashinori chant, in tsuyogin style. They are accompanied by percussion in flexible and the nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings.
Visuals
The shite and the waki kneel on Squares 9 and 6, respectively. For the final line of text, they first turn toward each other, before the shite turns back toward the audience.

Although the shite joins the jiutai in singing the sashi, the Intermedia Index is lowered because this non-congruent sashinori chant is accompanied by the percussion instruments in flexible and nohkan part in non-congruent rhythmic settings. The decrease in the Intermedia Index is also a result of the absence of any stage movement as the two protagonists remain motionless, almost until the end of the shōdan.

The text tells the story of another ancient Chinese sword, and while the shite and jiutai sing, they are not in dialogue since the jiutai simply completes the boy’s sentences. While the text of the next shōdan focuses on a legendary Japanese sword, the sashi’s text concludes the stories related to Chinese swords started in the preceding shōdan, the kuri. The sashi closes with an uptick of energy when the nohkan enters with the kuse no ryō pattern over the line, “and their miraculous power is beyond expression!” as the shite turns to make eye contact with the waki.
